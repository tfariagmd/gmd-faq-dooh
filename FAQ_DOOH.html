            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">What is Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH)?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) refers to digital advertising displays or screens placed in public locations. These screens are typically found in areas with high foot traffic, such as shopping malls, airports, transit stations, billboards, and sports stadiums. DOOH allows advertisers to display dynamic and engaging content, reaching a wide audience in various locations.</div>

        </div>

        <div class="faq-section">

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">How does DOOH work?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">DOOH works by utilizing digital screens and a content management system. Advertisers create and schedule their ad content using digital signage software or platforms. This content is then displayed on the digital screens, providing opportunities for targeted advertising, real-time updates, and interactive experiences. Some DOOH platforms also leverage programmatic advertising, allowing automated buying and selling of ad space in real time.</div>

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">How does the system count impressions or plays?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">The numbers are verified by COMMB (Canadian Out Of Home Marketing & Measurement Bureau). However, it is challenging to accurately forecast the conversion of one play to a specific number of impressions. This is because each media owner determines their own impression multipliers based on factors such as screen location, time of day, day of the week, venue type, and seasonal forecasts.


For instance, the impression multiplier for a billboard in downtown Toronto will likely be higher compared to a billboard in rural Saskatchewan due to differences in population density and viewer engagement. These variations in location-specific factors contribute to the different impression multipliers set by media owners.

</div>

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">Can we guarantee the number of impressions will be served on specific screens?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">Unfortunately, we cannot guarantee this as it depends on the availability of inventory during the campaign period. It's important to remember that we sell remnant inventory that these media owners have, so we cannot guarantee the exact amount of inventory available since we don't have direct access to it. However, we'll do our best to work with the available inventory and optimize your campaign for success.


In the programmatic advertising ecosystem, the inventory available for purchase is typically considered as a "remnant" inventory. This is because programmatic ads are served on ad space owned by publishers or media owners. As a buyer, we don't have direct control or awareness of the decisions made by the media owners regarding their inventory. For example, a media owner may choose to temporarily remove a screen from a digital out-of-home (DOOH) network for a period of time due to direct sales commitments they have made. Such decisions made by media owners can have an impact on our programmatic campaigns.

Similarly, if one of our direct clients buys a site takeover on a specific website, it means that other advertisers, often referred to as "remnant advertisers," will not be able to appear on that site during the period of the site takeover. 

</div>

        </div>

        <div class="faq-section">

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">Can we have direct access to the media owner inventory?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">Unfortunately, we cannot. It's similar to Whitelisting certain websites in Programmatic Display. While we are able to focus on selected screens, since we are not buying the inventory directly from the publisher/media owner, we aren't able to guarantee a specific number of impressions of individual screens.

Our DOOH product gives us the ability to access a wide variety of screens with our clients' ads, but since it's a programmatic product, we don't have the ability to book direct campaigns with individual media owners.

In the same way that we're not able to book campaigns directly on websites that Glacier doesn't own (or have a partnership with) -- such as CBC or TSN -- but our clients' ads are able to appear on these sites.

</div>

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">Can we stand in front of a screen hoping to see the ads?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">No, standing in front of a screen hoping to see specific ads is not an effective or reliable method. Digital out-of-home (DOOH) advertising is designed to reach a broader audience in public spaces rather than targeting individuals directly. The ads displayed on screens are typically delivered based on pre-determined schedules, algorithms, and targeting parameters set by the advertisers and media owners.


Attempting to view specific ads by standing in front of a screen may not yield the desired results, as the ads may rotate or change based on various factors such as time of day, location, and targeted audience segments. It's best to engage with DOOH ads as part of your regular activities in public spaces, rather than relying on personal attempts to view specific content.

</div>

            <div class="faq-question" onclick="toggleAnswer(this)">How does geofencing work with this product?</div>

            <div class="faq-answer">Similar to programmatic display, we are able to include virtual geofences in a campaign, where we will focus impressions on users whose devices have been previously tracked & collected within a set physical location. However, since a single play includes multiple impressions based on expected traffic around a screen when the client’s ad is shown, it is likely that even within the geofencing tactic, some impressions will be served to users who were not necessarily in one of the selected geofences. For instance, if we're tracking individuals entering the GMD office and then serving impressions wherever they are in BC, consider this scenario: I visited the GMD office and later went to a mall during my lunch break, where I entered an elevator with a screen. The system would recognize me as part of the desired audience and try to serve me the ad. However, there could be other people on the elevator who were not in the GMD office, and they would also be counted as impressions.

This highlights the distinction between Programmatic Display and DOOH. In Programmatic Display, impressions are exclusively delivered to individuals who have been in a geofence. This precision is achievable in programmatic display because impressions are directed to the specific user’s device.

Note that we are not able to report on conversion zones for DOOH.

        // Toggle FAQ answer visibility

        // Filter FAQs dynamically

        function filterFAQs() {

            const searchBox = document.getElementById('search-box').value.toLowerCase();

            faqs.forEach(faq => {

                if (question.includes(searchBox) || answer.includes(searchBox)) {

                    faq.style.display = '';

                } else {

                    faq.style.display = 'none';

                }

            });
